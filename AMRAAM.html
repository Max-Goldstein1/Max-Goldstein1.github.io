Roll Controlled Rocket

An attempt to achieve roll stabilization of a large high-powered rocket through actuated canards.

My friend and I built and twice launched an 8-foot scale model of an AIM-120 AMRAAM with actuated canards for our Barrett Honors Thesis. SDR's avionics team had developed a flight computer capable of such a task and we wanted to take up the challenge. Both launches failed to achieve roll authority due to airframe structural failures, but the recovery system performed properly each time. The project provided major lessons in composite manufacturing and safe high-powered flight operations.

Role & Responsibilities

  • Designed and built wet-layup fiberglass airframe and hand-cut fiberglass fins.
  • Designed nosecone fairing housing two downward-facing onboard cameras.
  • Integrated avionics including redundant parachute-deployment computers and a custom roll-control computer.
  • Packed recovery system and led launch operations.
