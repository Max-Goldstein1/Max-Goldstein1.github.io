Liquid Propulsion Lead

Arizona State University's first successfully fired liquid rocket engine.

Coming off the back of a second explosive hotfire test with our legacy engine, we decided to begin working on an entirely new design. I had the honor as liquid propulsion lead to guide the development of a 350 lbf LOX/IPA ablative engine. In addition to steering the project, I took ownership over the design, manufacture, and integration of the unlike triplet impingement injector. We ultimately achieved a nominal two second hotfire.

Role & Responsibilities

  • Led the design of a LOX/IPA ablative liquid rocket engine.
  • Designed unlike triplet impingement injector under tight geometric constraints.
  • Owned CAD, manufacturability redesign, and integration with the test stand.
  • Coordinated fill procedures, flow testing, troubleshooting, and hotfire operations.
