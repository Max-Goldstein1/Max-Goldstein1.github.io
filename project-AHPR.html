Advanced High Powered Rocketry Co-Lead

Two stage solid rocket to 44,000 feet at Mach 2.7

There was a desire within Sun Devil Rocketry to begin building bigger and faster rockets. Thomas Booska and I started a subteam with the goal of building up our manufacturing capability with composites, ultimately culminating in a fully student-built two-stage high-powered rocket. The vehicle featured COTS fiberglass airframes, fiberglass fins, dual redundant flight computers, a custom SDR flight computer, GPS, and onboard camera. The launch was successful with clean stage separation and second-stage ignition. The booster was recovered and the upper stage reached an apogee of 44,000 ft and Mach 2.7. The upper-stage parachute system failed to deploy due to suspected faulty black-powder charges.

Role & Responsibilities

  • Assisted in assembling airframe and fin fillets.
  • Updated OpenRocket stability model and performance predictions.
  • Avionics integration, parachute packing, and vehicle stacking during launch operations.
