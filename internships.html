Internships

Professional experience where I’ve applied what I’m learning to real-world problems.

United States Air Force, Civilian Service – Propulsion Engineer Intern

Edwards Air Force Base, CA • Summer 2024

During my internship at Edwards AFB, I developed a Python program to visualize angle of attack and sideslip limits as a function of Mach number for the T-7 Red Hawk. The tool generated coffin plots of aerodynamic limits across flight conditions, giving engineers a faster way to review and compare test data during envelope expansion. I also analyzed engine vibration measurements by applying analytical models to instrument readings to validate data received from third-party software. I was present in the control room during manned flight tests, where I saw firsthand the level of responsibility placed on engineers when their systems are intended to carry people.

Nammo Defense Systems – Shoulder Fired Systems Project Engineer Intern

Mesa, AZ • Summer 2023

I was a Project Engineer Intern at Nammo Defense Systems, working on the M72 FFE shoulder-fired launcher. I tested a more cost-effective alternative material for launcher components by conducting tensile and fluid compatibility tests and performing statistical data analysis. I also ran ANSYS structural simulations to validate that prototype parts would not exceed yield stress, and performed tolerance stackups while correcting engineering drawings through formal change proposals.

BlackBar Engineering – Engineering Intern

Sierra Vista, AZ • Summer 2022

At BlackBar Engineering, I assembled the tail boom and wing spars and vinyl-wrapped the wings of fixed-wing drones. I soldered and integrated electrical components for GPS and flight-computer test apparatuses, and 3D-modeled and printed prototypes of a thermal camera and battery housing. Working with this small team gave me an early, hands-on understanding of the aerospace business and end-to-end system integration.
